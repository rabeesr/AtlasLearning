# Why this topic matters

The first version of "intelligence" in a robot is search. Given a known map, how do you get from A to B? Given a graph of states, how do you find the cheapest path? These algorithms are the foundation of motion planning, task planning, and many flavors of decision-making.

## Deep-dive subtopics

- **C++ fundamentals.** Types, control flow, classes, RAII, the standard library.
- **Discrete graph mathematics.** Graphs, trees, complexity analysis.
- **Search algorithms.** BFS for shortest hops, Dijkstra for weighted shortest paths, A* with admissible heuristics, DFS for exhaustive enumeration.

## What comes next

Search is reused across the curriculum: motion planning, SLAM front-ends, task scheduling, and even some perception pipelines.
